Why this matters for L&D: Dimension 1 is squarely in our lane — and it's the one that produces the largest multiplier effect. The study frames "comprehensive upskilling" explicitly as behavioral change, not seat time or completion rates. That's the argument: L&D's value isn't in building the course. It's in closing the gap between knowing and doing.
